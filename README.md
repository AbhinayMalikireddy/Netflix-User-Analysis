# Netflix-User-Analysis
## Project Background
Initialy Netflix is website, as a DVD rental service in 1998, later on it started streaming in 2007 and they lanuched Netflix globaly in 2016 via website and mobile app.

This project explores the key reasons behind Netflix’s declining user base. It analyzes trends such as rising subscription costs, increasing competition from other streaming platforms, limited regional content, and changes in user viewing preferences. By examining customer behavior, market shifts, and platform performance, the project aims to identify the major factors contributing to user churn and provide insights that can help improve retention strategies.

## Executive Summary 

#### Overview Findings

This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of Netflix’s user performance across multiple years, highlighting trends in revenue, subscription behavior, viewing patterns, and genre preferences. It shows that revenue peaked in 2023 and 2024, while 2022 recorded the lowest performance. The subscription analysis reveals strong user engagement in Premium and Standard plans during the peak years. The viewing pattern chart indicates that evenings are the most active time for movie recommendations, suggesting optimal engagement windows. Additionally, the genre analysis identifies high-potential categories such as Thriller, Sports, and Mystery, offering opportunities for strategic content expansion.

- Revenue peaked in 2023 and 2024, reaching $66.14K and $63.47K respectively. However, 2022 recorded Netflix’s lifetime low revenue at $26.67K. Even during the pandemic—when most streaming platforms experienced rapid growth in subscribers and watch-time—Netflix lagged behind, indicating weaker performance compared to competitors.
- Across all years, Premium and Standard plans consistently attracted the highest number of users. This suggests strong customer preference for feature-rich plans, while Basic and Premium+ plans require more focus to improve adoption.
- 2023 and 2024 were the best-performing years in terms of both revenue and subscription volume. Even in 2022, despite poor overall performance, Premium and Standard plans still outperformed other tiers, highlighting their continued popularity.
- A sharp decline in 2025 is visible across both revenue and subscriptions. This drop may be attributed to subscriber loss, price increases, reduced content quality, or growing competition, all of which could impact user retention.

- Revenue significantly increased in 2023, reaching its peak, while monthly growth % dropped from 2022 to 2023 indicating high revenue despite slower growth, possibly due to existing subscribers renewing or upgrading plans.
- In 2024, revenue remained stable and high, but monthly growth % stayed flat around 7-8%, suggesting that Netflix maintained strong earning power but struggled to bring in new users.
- 2025 shows a steep decline in both revenue and monthly growth %, which fell close to zero. This indicates major issues such as subscriber loss, market saturation, or reduced engagement.
- User count lose sharply from 2022 to early 2023, showing strong onboarding and higher engagement, Throughout 2023 and 2024, user counts remained consistently high (800–900 users per quarter), indicating stable platform usage and strong retention during these years.

## Recommendations

Based on the uncovered insights, the following recommendations provided:
- Increase investments in trending genres such as Thriller, Sports, and Mystery, which show strong user interest. Expand fresh and engaging content in these categories to boost retention.
- Introduce targeted retention strategies like loyalty benefits, personalized recommendations, and discount offers for long-term users to reduce churn.
- Conduct regional price sensitivity analysis before changing subscription prices. Test smaller adjustments instead of step hikes.
- Enhance adaptive streaming technology, reduce buffering, and ensure smooth performance even on low bandwidth.
- Increase language options, subtitles, and region-specific content which can capture wider demographics.
